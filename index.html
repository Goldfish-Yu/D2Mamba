Abstract

Curvilinear structure segmentation—encompassing retinal vessels, nerve fibers, road networks, and sperm tails—is a fundamental problem in medical and remote-sensing image analysis. Existing CNN-based methods are limited by their local receptive fields, while Vision Transformers suffer from quadratic computational complexity, both struggling to capture the global structural continuity intrinsic to curvilinear shapes. We propose D2Mamba, a Dual Dynamic State Space Model that introduces two complementary selective scanning streams: a directional multi-scale scan that traces structural continuity along curvilinear paths, and a frequency-aware dynamic scan that exploits texture-level discriminability at fine scales. The two streams are fused via a learnable gating mechanism, yielding rich representations with linear computational complexity. We further introduce SpermSeg, a challenging new benchmark with pixel-level annotations for sperm head, midpiece, and tail regions. Extensive experiments on SpermSeg and four public benchmarks show that D2Mamba achieves state-of-the-art performance while maintaining competitive efficiency.
